# Product Brief: CashOut

## The Problem

Cash is invisible. Every swipe of a card leaves a digital trail — but the $40 you pulled from the ATM? Gone. Spent on coffee, parking, lunch, a tip here, a snack there. By the end of the week, neither you nor your partner can account for where it went.

For couples who use cash regularly, this creates a blind spot in their finances — and money is the number-one source of relationship conflict. Existing expense tracking apps are built around bank-linked transactions and digital payments. They treat cash as an afterthought — a manual entry buried behind complex budgeting workflows that take 30+ seconds per transaction. The result: people try tracking cash, get frustrated by the friction, and quit within weeks.

The money keeps disappearing. The conversations about "where did it all go?" keep happening.

## The Solution

**CashOut** is an iOS app purpose-built for one thing: making cash spending visible between partners — instantly.

The core experience is radical simplicity:

1. **3 taps. 4 seconds. Done.** Open the app, tap a category, enter the amount, save. No account selection, no budgeting philosophy to learn first. If a feature makes entry take longer, it doesn't ship.
2. **Shared visibility in real-time.** Both partners see the same spending feed. No syncing, no exporting, no awkward "let me show you my app" moments.
3. **Insights that land.** Daily, weekly, and monthly breakdowns by category reveal where cash actually goes — surfacing the "oh wow, *that's* where the money is going" moment that drives better spending decisions.

## What Makes CashOut Different

The market is full of budgeting apps. None of them nail this specific intersection:

| | CashOut | General Budget Apps (YNAB, Monarch) | Couple Apps (Honeydue) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cash-first design** | Built for it | Afterthought | Not a focus |
| **Entry speed** | < 5 seconds | 15-30+ seconds | 10-20 seconds |
| **Couples sharing** | Native, real-time | Add-on feature | Yes, but bank-linked |
| **Learning curve** | None | Steep (budgeting philosophy) | Moderate |
| **Privacy** | No bank credentials needed | Requires bank linking | Requires bank linking |
| **Price** | Free | $15/mo | Free (buggy) |

**Narrowness is the moat.** CashOut doesn't compete with full budgeting suites — and never will. It solves one problem exceptionally well, for one audience. That focus is what makes the speed and simplicity possible. Every competitor is trying to be everything; CashOut does one thing better than all of them.

**Privacy by design.** CashOut will never ask for bank passwords. No Plaid integration, no credential sharing, no data-breach surface area. In a post-breach landscape, this isn't a limitation — it's a trust advantage.

## Target Users

**Primary:** Couples (cohabiting or married) who use cash regularly and want shared visibility into where it goes. They aren't looking for a complete financial management platform — they want a quick, focused tool that answers "where is our cash going?"

**User profile:**
- Use cash deliberately (for budgeting discipline, privacy, tipping, small purchases)
- Smartphone-native (iOS 26+)
- Value simplicity over feature depth
- Want financial transparency with their partner without the friction of spreadsheets or "budget meetings"

## MVP Scope

**In scope for v1:**
- Ultra-fast expense entry (category + amount + optional note)
- Predefined and custom spending categories
- Shared household — both partners see all entries in real-time
- Daily, weekly, and monthly spending views with category breakdowns
**v2 candidates:**
- Budget/goal setting per category
- Receipt photo capture
- Cash withdrawal tracking with reconciliation
- Smart reminders to log spending

**Explicitly out of scope:**
- Bank account linking (permanent design decision, not a deferral)
- Multi-currency support
- App Store public launch (v1 is personal use)

**Non-goals:** CashOut will never become a full budgeting suite. It will never link to bank accounts. It will never run ads.

## Success Criteria

CashOut succeeds when:

1. **Consistent daily use** — both partners log at least one cash purchase per day, sustaining the habit beyond the first two weeks (the typical drop-off point for manual tracking apps)
2. **Spending awareness** — the weekly/monthly views reveal patterns that were previously invisible, producing at least one "I didn't realize we spent that much on X" moment per week
3. **Better conversations** — cash discussions between partners shift from "where did the money go?" to informed, specific, data-driven conversations

## Key Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| **Manual entry fatigue** (the #1 killer of cash tracking apps) | Obsessive focus on entry speed — category shortcuts, smart defaults, and a flow that genuinely takes < 5 seconds. If usability testing shows average entry > 5 seconds, redesign before shipping. |
| **Habit formation** — users abandon tracking within weeks | Make the insight views rewarding enough to sustain motivation. Reminders planned for v2. |
| **Asymmetric adoption** — one partner logs, the other doesn't | Design the invite flow and shared feed to make both partners feel ownership. Keep the barrier to entry as low as possible for the second partner. |
| **Scope creep** — temptation to become a full budgeting app | Maintain the "one thing, done well" philosophy. If a feature doesn't make cash tracking faster or insights clearer, it doesn't belong. |

## Vision

CashOut is a financial intimacy tool disguised as a cash tracker. It reduces money friction between two people who share a life — turning invisible spending into visible, shared understanding.

The deliberate cash-use movement (cash stuffing, envelope budgeting) is growing, and no purpose-built tool exists at this intersection of speed, simplicity, and shared visibility. Start small. Nail the habit. Let the insights speak for themselves.
